# Varholdt AI Outreach Strategy

## ICP Read

The business plan's ICP is directionally right: document-heavy SMBs with a named workflow owner, recurring document volume, and a buyer who can approve a $5K sprint quickly.

The sharpest wedge is not "AI for businesses." It is:

> We turn one recurring document workflow into a human-reviewed AI workflow agent that lives where your team already works.

For the first 50, the strongest segments are:

1. Proposal/capture consultants
   - Best partner channel.
   - They already know GovCon capture and proposal strategy.
   - Varholdt AI should not replace their expertise; it should build the workflow layer underneath it.
   - Fastest path to borrowed trust.

2. Federal-adjacent ops/proposal teams
   - Strong founder credibility because of veteran status, DOL work, and OmniBid.
   - The pitch must stay in document workflow territory: RFPs, SOWs, compliance matrices, past-performance docs, deliverables.
   - Avoid "win more contracts" language.

3. Staffing and recruiting
   - Very clean workflow pain: resumes, client submissions, screening notes, onboarding packets, credential docs.
   - Easier to understand than GovCon for a first paid sprint.
   - Local/regional agencies are reachable and likely owner-operated.

4. Insurance agencies
   - Strong repeated document flows: COIs, renewals, claims support, quote intake, carrier comparisons.
   - The pitch should be operational: "same-day certificate and renewal prep workflows," not broad AI transformation.

5. CPA/accounting firms
   - Clear document volume and seasonal pressure.
   - Harder sales timing unless tied to tax-season intake, monthly close, or client portal triage.

## Outreach Rules

- One message per company, aimed at the most likely workflow owner.
- Mention one observed workflow from the source page.
- Ask for the free 30-minute workflow audit.
- Do not pitch the $6K/month retainer cold.
- Do not attach a long deck.
- Use the OmniBid Loom only when the target is GovCon/proposal-adjacent; for staffing, insurance, and CPA, mention it as proof only if needed.

## Segment-Specific Hooks

Proposal/capture:
"You already own the capture/proposal judgment. I build the document workflow layer underneath it: RFP intake, compliance matrices, source-traceable requirement extraction, and review queues."

GovCon ops/proposal:
"I am not a capture strategist. I build human-reviewed agents for the document work around solicitations, SOWs, amendments, compliance matrices, and deliverables."

Staffing:
"Resume intake, screening notes, client submission packets, and onboarding docs are exactly the kind of repeated document workflow where an AI assistant can draft and route, while recruiters approve."

Insurance:
"COIs, renewal prep, claims support, quote intake, and carrier comparisons are repeated document workflows. The goal is not to replace account managers; it is to remove the copy/paste and routing work around them."

CPA/accounting:
"Client portals collect the files, but someone still has to classify, chase, reconcile, and route the work. That is the workflow to audit."

## Success Metric For This List

Run the 50 as a learning campaign, not a vanity CRM build.

- Good: 5+ replies, 3+ discovery calls, 1 paid sprint candidate.
- Strong: 8+ replies, 5+ discovery calls, 1 paid sprint sold.
- Weak signal: fewer than 3 replies after all 50, assuming messages are personalized and sent to a real owner/operator channel.

If replies are weak, fix the message and channel before changing the offer.
