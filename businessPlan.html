📌What this doc is
The complete operating plan for Varholdt AI. Designed to be handed off to another agent — or picked up by Layken in two weeks — and executed without re-deliberation. If a question isn't answered here, the default is: stop writing, send the next outreach message, and let real buyer conversations resolve it.

What Changed from v4

  • Repositioned from "AI Workflow Engineering" to Fractional AI Department for document-heavy businesses. Buyers understand "department."
  • Sprint + retainer replaces Teardown + Build + Launch Support as the primary offer. Custom builds remain on the menu but are no longer the main pitch.
  • ICP widened from "GovCon primary" to document-heavy broadly (GovCon, proposal consultants, staffing, insurance, CPA, construction back office).
  • Week 1 reduced to four assets and an outreach list. LLC/EIN/insurance push to Week 2.
  • Founding sprint price: $5,000 credited toward a 6-month retainer if signed within 14 days.
  • No SaaS for 90 days. OmniBid stays as a demo asset only.
  • CUI/CMMC deployment tier framework retained — credibility moat in GovCon discovery.
  • Anti-pivot rule added: this is the last rewrite before a buyer conversation happens.

We install a practical AI department inside document-heavy businesses — without you hiring a full AI team. We find the workflows worth automating, build them, train your team, and keep them running.

Most businesses your size know AI matters but do not know which tools to trust, which workflows are worth automating, or who to hire. Hiring a full-time AI engineer is a $180K+ commitment with no guarantee of ROI.

Part 3The Offer Stack

Three offers. One front door, one main offer, one exception.

Offer 1 — AI Workflow Sprint (front door)

$5,000 flat

Duration: 10 business days · One-time · 100% credited toward 6-month retainer if signed within 14 days

Deliverables

  1. Current-state map of one document-heavy workflow
  2. Working prototype of the highest-leverage slice (live, demo-able)
  3. 90-day implementation roadmap
  4. Recommendation memo: buy / build / integrate + ROI assumptions
  5. Live walkthrough / leadership training (~60 min, recorded)
Why it exists
Buyers won't commit $36K–$60K to a fractional AI engagement based on a portfolio. The sprint lets them feel the work, see a working artifact, and decide on month 2+ from evidence rather than faith.

Offer 3 — Build-Only Project (exception, not main pitch)

$20K–$60K flat

When to offer: Only when a prospect knows exactly what they want, has been through a similar build, and scope is provably narrow. Buyers who want a defined deliverable, not a relationship.

Why de-emphasized: Retainer model produces better client outcomes (workflows need tuning post-deploy) and better business outcomes (predictable revenue, compounding case studies, less scope-creep risk).

Pricing Posture

  • Founding prices exist to buy proof. Two founding clients, then prices step up.
  • No further discounting below founding price.
  • If a prospect can't afford $6K/month for a 30-person business with real document pain, they aren't the ICP.
  • Sprint paid up front. Retainer paid monthly, first month due at kickoff.

Anti-ICP — Hard No's

Discovery Call Qualifying Questions

Ask in order. If 3+ are uncertain, this is not a founding engagement candidate.

  1. What document-heavy workflow slows your team down every week?
  2. Who owns that workflow today?
  3. How many times per week or month does it happen?
  4. What systems and documents are involved?
  5. What have you tried to solve it?
  6. What would make a solution obviously worth $6K/month to you?
  7. Would you rather pay monthly for an ongoing partner, or fixed price for a defined project? (After 5 calls, rebalance offer if 3+ lean fixed-price.)
  8. Who else needs to be in the room to make this decision?
  9. What's your timeline for deciding?
  10. (GovCon only) Do any documents contain CUI, or material covered by DFARS 252.204-7012, ITAR, or your prime's CMMC flowdown? (Routes to deployment tier — see Part 8.)

10 business days. Fixed scope. Fixed price. Fixed deliverables.

  • Decision-maker name
  • Workflow owner name

  • Success criteria
  • Deployment tier for prototype (Part 8)

DAYS 2–4

Discovery

  • 2–4 stakeholder interviews / screenshares
  • Observe the actual work being done (not what they say they do — what they do)
  • Map current state: triggers, systems, handoffs, documents, people, time per cycle, error rates, exceptions
  • Identify the highest-leverage slice (20% that produces 80% of pain)

DAYS 4–7

Prototype

  • Build working prototype of highest-leverage slice
  • Sanitized, public, or approved sample documents only
  • Demo-able: upload → process → review → export
  • Source citations and confidence scores where applicable
  • Audit log

DAY 8

Internal review & ROI model

  • Write buy/build/integrate recommendation
  • Build ROI estimate with explicit assumptions
  • Draft 90-day roadmap
  • Draft 6-month retainer proposal

DAY 9

Training session

  • 60-minute leadership training, recorded
  • Topics: what AI is good at, what it isn't, how the prototype works, what month 2+ looks like, what to expect from a 6-month engagement

DAY 10

Delivery

  • PDF deliverable (workflow map, prototype link, ROI, roadmap, recommendation)
  • Live demo to decision-maker and workflow owner

Most Tier 1/2 clients don't have an internal team to host or operate workflows. Default model: you host on Varholdt AI accounts; their domain points at your hosting; you operate the system. API keys live on your accounts. Hosting + tokens bundled into retainer up to a cap, pass-through above.

Tier 3 (CUI / GovCloud) is the exception — they own the hosting boundary by regulatory necessity, so usage runs on their accounts with no pass-through.

Part 11Risks & How They're Handled

1. Doc-rewriting addiction (biggest risk)

The pattern of rewriting the founding doc instead of executing it has already happened five times. Rule: this is the last rewrite before a buyer conversation. Subsequent revisions only after 50 outreach attempts have been made.

2. No conversations after 30 outreach attempts

Diagnosis order: (1) target list quality, (2) outreach message, (3) offer. Do not jump to (3). Re-examine (1) and (2) first with a specific friend or advisor.

3. First sprint client provides no access

Mitigation: SOW requires sample docs and 2–4 stakeholder interviews. If client cannot provide these in Day 1 kickoff, the sprint pauses.

4. Scope creep

One workflow per sprint. Fixed SOW. New requests = bugs (fix), change requests (out of scope), or new SOWs (quote separately). No exceptions, including for friends.

5. AI cost surprises

Pass-through above cap. Monitor weekly. Recalculate caps after sprint #1 against real workflow data.

6. FDEs from AI labs

Not a near-term threat for the ICP. Anthropic and OpenAI FDEs target Fortune 500. The 40-person SDVOSB and the 80-person regional insurance agency are not their customers and won't be for years.

7. SaaS commoditization of generic workflows

Mitigation: Buy/build/integrate stays a core principle. Recommend SaaS when SaaS fits. Win where workflows are specific, cross-system, human-reviewed, and poorly served by generic tools.

8. Solo bandwidth crush

Hard cap at one active sprint OR one active retainer build at a time until delivery process is proven. Do not sign retainer #2 until retainer #1 is past month 2 and stable.

9. Income volatility

Keep DOL job until 6 months of family expenses in cash AND meaningful recurring revenue (retainer revenue ≥ $10K/mo with at least one renewed client).

10. Liability event

Mitigation: human-in-the-loop on every deployed workflow, audit logs, decision-support framing (never autonomous high-stakes decisions), E&O coverage, contract liability caps.
