**Varholdt AI is a Fractional AI Department for document-heavy small and mid-sized businesses. We build human-reviewed AI workflow agents that live in the tools your team already uses.**

We are the alternative: a senior AI operator who shows up part-time for 6 months, finds the bottlenecks, builds the agents, trains your team, and keeps it running. The agents live in the tools you already pay for, with human review on every output and full audit trail. When the engagement ends, you own working systems and your team knows how to use them.

**Positioning note (do not put this on the website, but use it to keep the framing tight):** Cyndra for document-heavy SMB workflows — founder-led implementation, stronger human-review and citation-traceability discipline, narrower than a generic "AI employees" play.

## Part 3 — The Offer Stack

Four offers, four roles:

1. **Free 30-min Workflow Audit** — top-of-funnel qualifier. The thing every cold outreach links to.
2. **$5K AI Workflow Sprint** — paid proof. The thing that produces a real prototype.
3. **Fractional AI Department retainer** — the main commercial outcome.
4. **Build-Only project** — exception case, not pitched in outreach.

Sprint and retainer are the only two offers *pitched* in outreach. The audit is the *call-to-action* that gets prospects into a conversation where the sprint can be scoped.

### Offer 0 — Free 30-Minute AI Workflow Audit (front door / CTA)

- **Price:** Free.
- **Format:** 30-minute video call. No deliverable, no written report, no prototype. A sales motion, not a product.
- **What happens on the call:**
  - 10 min — prospect describes one document-heavy workflow that slows their team down
  - 10 min — verbal feedback: what's likely automatable, what isn't, where the highest-leverage slice probably sits, whether SaaS already solves it
  - 10 min — qualify pain, workflow owner, volume, urgency, data access, decision timeline; pitch the sprint if there's fit
- **Discipline:**
  - Do not build anything before, during, or after this call. No slides, no maps, no follow-up document. A summary email at most.
  - If a prospect tries to extract free strategy ("can you send me a list of tools that would work?"), the answer is "that's exactly what the $5K sprint produces — written, with a working prototype."
- **Why it exists:** Reduces friction from cold message to first conversation. Cyndra-style. Lets Layken get more reps in front of real workflows faster, which also helps close the GovCon domain-depth gap by exposure rather than self-study.
- **Why it's safe:** The sprint is still the conversion offer. The audit doesn't replace the sprint — it sits above it. Pricing power is intact because the audit doesn't ship a real deliverable.

### Offer 1 — AI Workflow Sprint (paid proof)

- **Price:** $5,000 flat. One-time. 10 business days.
- **Credit mechanic:** 100% credited toward a 6-month Fractional AI Department retainer if signed within 14 days of sprint delivery.
- **Deliverables:**
  1. Current-state map of **one** document-heavy workflow (adjacent bottlenecks may be noted, but only one is scoped)
  2. Working prototype of the highest-leverage slice (live, demo-able, not slideware)
  3. 90-day implementation roadmap
  4. Recommendation memo: buy / build / integrate + ROI assumptions (sometimes the answer is "use this SaaS tool, here's the integration") — see Part 7
  5. Live walkthrough / leadership training session (~60 minutes, recorded)
- **Why it exists:** Buyers will not commit $36K–$60K to a fractional AI engagement based on a portfolio. The sprint lets them feel the work, see a working artifact, and decide on month 2+ from a position of evidence rather than faith.

### Offer 2 — Fractional AI Department (main offer)

- **Founding price:** $6,000/month, 6-month minimum. First 2 clients only.
- **Standard price (after 2 case studies):** $10,000/month, 6-month minimum.
- **Founding economics:** $5K sprint. If client continues within 14 days, the $5K credits toward month 1 of the retainer. Total paid over the full 6-month relationship: ~$36,000.
- **Standard economics:** $5K sprint, credited the same way. $10K/month × 6. Total paid: ~$60,000.
- **What we typically ship in 6 months** (use in sales copy):
  - 1 core workflow system shipped and tuned to production
  - 1–2 supporting automations or integrations
  - Monthly team training (live or recorded)
  - Monthly ROI / usage report
  - Quarterly roadmap review
  - Ongoing support, tuning, and prompt iteration on shipped work
- **How time is allocated** (lives in the SOW, not in outreach copy): ~10 hrs/mo senior AI strategy (calls, architecture, vendor selection, roadmap), ~20 hrs/mo implementation (builds, integrations, prompt engineering, deployment).
- **Operating constraints:**
  - Async support via Slack/email (same-business-day response, no nights/weekends/SLA)
  - One active workflow build at a time (no parallel scope creep)
  - Hosting, infrastructure, and AI tokens included up to a monthly cap; pass-through at cost above cap (see Part 8)
  - Buy/build/integrate guidance (no obligation to build custom when SaaS fits)
  - Human-review guardrails on every deployed workflow
- **Excluded:**
  - 24/7 on-call
  - Uptime SLAs
  - CMMC vendor-flowdown certification work
  - Highly sensitive / classified data handling
  - Strategic decisions the client should be making
  - *(These exclusions also apply to the Continuing Operations tier — see Part 8.)*

### Offer 3 — Build-Only Project (exception, not main pitch)

- **Price:** $20,000–$60,000 flat, scope-dependent.
- **When to offer:** Only when a prospect already knows exactly what they want, has been through a similar build before, and the scope is provably narrow. This is the exception case for buyers who want a defined deliverable and not a relationship.
- **Why it's de-emphasized:** The retainer model produces better client outcomes (because workflows need tuning after deployment) and better business outcomes (predictable revenue, compounding case studies, less scope-creep risk).

### Pricing posture

- Founding prices exist to buy proof. Two founding clients, then prices step up.
- No further discounting below founding price. If a prospect cannot afford $6K/month for a 30-person business with real document pain, they are not the ICP.
- Sprint is paid up front. Retainer is paid monthly, first month due at kickoff.

## Part 4 — ICP and Niche

### Primary ICP — Document-heavy small to mid-sized businesses

The widened ICP for the first 50 outreach attempts. We are looking for any business where humans repeatedly read, extract, route, draft, or verify document information.

**Target profile:**

- 20–250 employees
- $3M–$75M revenue
- A workflow owner (person who feels the pain and has authority over the process)
- A decision-maker who can sign a $5K invoice within 30 days
- Recurring document/email/form volume (not one-off projects)
- Has tried SaaS but the messy middle remains

**Sub-segments to test in the first 50:**

1. **Federal-adjacent contractors (proposal and ops teams, not capture strategy)** — RFPs, SOWs, compliance matrices, capability statements, past performance. We sell **document workflow expertise**, not capture/proposal-management expertise. Veteran + active federal contractor + OmniBid demo provide credibility on "I understand the federal world and federal documents" — not on "I know how to win contracts." If a discovery call drifts toward capture strategy, pricing strategy, or proposal management, refer out (see partner channel note below).
2. **Proposal consultants and capture firms** — They already own the capture/strategy domain. We build the workflow layer underneath their service. **Partner channel target:** they bring domain depth; we bring the document-AI implementation. Either as referral source (they recommend us to their GovCon clients) or as direct client (we tool *their* proposal shop).
3. **Staffing and recruiting** — Resume intake, candidate-job matching, compliance packets, client submissions.
4. **Regional insurance agencies** — Claims intake, document extraction, renewal prep, response drafting. Strong Lafayette/Acadiana density.
5. **CPA and accounting firms** — Document intake, source doc extraction, working papers, client communication drafting.
6. **Construction back office** — Submittals, RFIs, change orders, vendor docs.

**Positioning discipline on segment 1:**
- ✅ "We help proposal and operations teams turn RFPs, SOWs, amendments, and past-performance docs into reviewable AI-assisted workflows."
- ❌ "We help GovCon firms win more contracts." (Implies capture, pricing, proposal management — not our lane.)

**First-50 weighting:** Focus on segments where credibility is strongest and outreach language is sharpest. Reweighted in v6 to acknowledge the GovCon domain-depth gap — fewer cold pitches into GovCon ops teams until either (a) Layken closes the knowledge gap via APMP Foundation or shadowing a real proposal cycle, or (b) we route GovCon work through proposal-consultant partners.

- **15 federal-adjacent ops/proposal teams** (down from 25). Lead with document workflow language, never capture strategy.
- **15 proposal / capture consultants** (up from 10). Higher weighting because they're both direct buyers and the partner channel that backstops the GovCon knowledge gap.
- **10 staffing / recruiting firms** (unchanged).
- **5 regional insurance agencies** (unchanged).
- **5 CPA / accounting firms** (promoted from warm-intros only).
- Construction back office held for warm intros only until one of the five active segments produces signal.

**ICP narrowing rule:** After the first 50 outreach attempts (expected: weeks 1–3), the segment with the highest reply rate and most qualified discovery calls becomes the primary cold-outbound target. Other segments stay open through warm intros only.

### Anti-ICP — Hard no's

- Pre-product startups looking for a CTO
- Companies under 20 employees with no real workflow volume
- Large enterprises with formal procurement and 6-month vendor onboarding
- Buyers whose first question is whether the sprint price is negotiable below $5K
- Companies expecting AI to replace human judgment on high-stakes decisions
- Engagements requiring formal CMMC vendor-flowdown certification (SSP, POA&M, audit trail, personnel screening) for me as a vendor inside their compliance boundary
- Classified or ITAR-restricted workflows
- Any buyer who cannot identify the workflow owner in the first call
- Any buyer who cannot provide sample documents or process access in the sprint
- Buying decisions that cannot close inside 30 days

### Discovery call qualifying questions

Ask these in order. If three or more are uncertain, this is not a founding engagement candidate.

1. What document-heavy workflow slows your team down every week?
2. Who owns that workflow today?
3. How many times per week or month does it happen?
4. What systems and documents are involved?
5. What have you tried to solve it?
6. What would make a solution obviously worth $6K/month to you?
7. Would you rather pay monthly for an ongoing partner, or pay a fixed price for a defined project? *(Listening for retainer-vs-fixed-price signal. After 5 calls, rebalance the main offer if 3+ lean fixed-price.)*
8. Who else needs to be in the room to make this decision?
9. What's your timeline for deciding?
10. **(GovCon only)** Do any documents in this workflow contain Controlled Unclassified Information, or material covered by DFARS 252.204-7012, ITAR, or your prime's CMMC flowdown requirements? *(Routes to deployment tier — see Part 8.)*

## Part 6 — Sales Assets

Four assets. Built in Week 1. Iterated based on what conversations reveal.

### The Workflow Agents Catalog

Six named agents we lead with in sales. Each one is described in a four-line spec so the buyer can see themselves in it. Use this structure on the landing page, in the PDF, and on calls. **Do not invent agents we cannot ship in a sprint** — every agent below maps to capability that already exists in OmniBid, Civicly, Atlas, or the implementation patterns we've shipped.

1. **RFP Response Agent**
   - **Reads:** RFP PDFs, amendments, Q&A, past-performance library
   - **Does:** extracts L/M/C requirements with section citations, flags deadlines and missing capabilities, drafts a compliance matrix
   - **Human approves:** requirements interpretation, assumptions, no-bid recommendations
   - **Outputs to:** Excel / Word / Teams / SharePoint proposal folder

2. **Document Intake Agent**
   - **Reads:** inbound emails, attachments, scanned forms, source docs
   - **Does:** classifies the document, extracts structured fields with confidence scores, routes to the right queue or system
   - **Human approves:** low-confidence extractions and exception cases
   - **Outputs to:** CRM, ATS, claims system, accounting software, or a review queue

3. **Resume Screening Agent**
   - **Reads:** inbound resumes, the open requisition, the company's hiring rubric
   - **Does:** scores candidate-job match, surfaces top N candidates with rationale, drafts initial outreach
   - **Human approves:** the shortlist and the outreach copy before send
   - **Outputs to:** ATS, recruiter inbox, hiring-manager Slack/Teams channel

4. **Claims Prep Agent**
   - **Reads:** claim submission, supporting documents, policy file
   - **Does:** extracts the loss event, identifies the applicable coverage clauses, drafts the initial coverage summary
   - **Human approves:** coverage determination, exclusions called out, response language
   - **Outputs to:** claims system, adjuster inbox, response template

5. **Drafting Workbench Agent**
   - **Reads:** prior proposals, capability statements, the new ask, house voice guide
   - **Does:** pre-drafts proposal sections, vendor letters, RFI responses in the team's voice
   - **Human approves:** every paragraph before it leaves
   - **Outputs to:** Word, Google Docs, proposal template

6. **Knowledge Search Agent**
   - **Reads:** past performance, SOPs, prior proposals, internal wiki
   - **Does:** answers natural-language questions with grounded citations to source documents
   - **Human approves:** N/A (answers are read-only; citation links let the user verify)
   - **Outputs to:** Slack, Teams, or a lightweight web search UI

**Where the agent lives ("delivery surface"):** Default delivery is whichever existing tool the team already lives in — Outlook / Gmail, Slack / Teams, SharePoint / Drive, the CRM, the ATS, Excel. A lightweight web app is added only where review and control specifically require one (e.g., a review queue UI with confidence highlighting and audit log). Promise: **every sprint has one primary interface and one export destination — no Zapier-in-a-trench-coat integration sprawl.**

The accurate positioning is: Vercel AI SDK gives us the agent/runtime layer, tool calling, and MCP client support; Vercel Chat SDK gives us a practical multi-chat delivery layer; app APIs, MCP servers, and automation bridges give us the actual system connections. ## Part 7 — Delivery: The Sprint

10 business days. Fixed scope. Fixed price. Fixed deliverables.

### Day 1 — Kickoff (90 minutes)

Confirm in writing:

- Workflow in scope (one workflow, not three)
- Decision-maker name
- Workflow owner name
- 2–4 stakeholders for interviews
- Sample documents access (sanitized, public, or explicitly approved)
- Success criteria
- Deployment tier for prototype (Part 8)

### Days 2–4 — Discovery

- 2–4 stakeholder interviews / screenshares
- Observe the actual work being done (not what they say they do — what they do)
- Map current state: triggers, systems, handoffs, documents, people, time per cycle, error rates, exception cases
- Identify the highest-leverage slice (the 20% that produces 80% of the pain)

### Days 4–7 — Prototype

- Build the working prototype of the highest-leverage slice
- Sanitized, public, or approved sample documents only
- Demo-able: upload → process → review → export
- Source citations and confidence scores where applicable
- Audit log

### Day 8 — Internal review and ROI model

- Write the buy/build/integrate recommendation
- Build the ROI estimate with explicit assumptions
- Draft the 90-day roadmap
- Draft the 6-month retainer proposal

### Day 9 — Training session

- 60-minute leadership training, recorded
- Topics: what AI is good at, what it isn't, how this prototype works, what month 2+ would look like, what to expect from a 6-month engagement

### Day 10 — Delivery

- PDF deliverable (workflow map, prototype demo link, ROI, roadmap, recommendation)
- Live demo to decision-maker and workflow owner
- Retainer proposal handed over with 14-day credit window
- Case study permission requested in SOW

## Part 8 — Operations, Pricing Mechanics, and Compliance

### Infrastructure & usage caps

Most Tier 1/2 clients do not have an internal team to host or operate the workflows you build. Default model: **you host on Varholdt AI accounts; their domain points at your hosting; you operate the system.** API keys live on your accounts. Hosting + tokens are bundled into the retainer up to a cap, with pass-through above cap.

Tier 3 (CUI / GovCloud) is the exception — they own the hosting boundary by regulatory necessity, so usage runs on their accounts and there is no pass-through.

Initial planning assumptions only — recalculate against first real workflow's volume after sprint #1.

**Initial assumption (single document-extraction workflow, ~50 docs/day):**

- Extraction (Gemini Flash, ~10K in / ~1K out per doc): $50–100/mo
- Review/drafting (Claude Sonnet, half the docs): $300–600/mo
- Hosting (Convex / Vercel / storage): $50–200/mo
- 20–30% buffer for retries, embeddings, edge cases
- **Expected total:** $450–1,100/mo

**Plan caps:**

- Sprint prototype: hosting and tokens included within reason.
- Fractional AI Department retainer (Tier 1/2): hosting + tokens included up to a $1,500/month combined cap. Above cap = pass-through at cost. Explicit in SOW.
- Fractional AI Department retainer (Tier 3): client owns hosting and API accounts; usage runs on their infrastructure; no pass-through from Varholdt AI.
- Continuing Operations: covered separately in the next subsection.

### Deployment tiers (GovCon discovery routes here)

Three tiers based on the CUI/CMMC qualifying question.

**Tier 1 — No CUI exposure.** Most workflows. Default managed hosting on my stack. SOW explicitly limits scope to non-CUI documents. Standard pricing.

**Tier 2 — CUI exposure, client lacks GovCloud.** Deploy to client-owned commercial infrastructure with CUI exclusion in SOW, OR scope workflow to non-CUI portions. Pricing reflects added setup work.

**Tier 3 — CUI must be handled compliantly.** Deploy to client-owned GovCloud or Azure Government. Higher implementation cost. Screen aggressively for fit. Only when client owns their compliance boundary and is buying me as implementer, not as a vendor inside their CMMC flowdown chain.

**Out of scope (year 1):** Formal CMMC vendor-flowdown certification work where I'm a vendor inside the client's compliance boundary (SSP, POA&M, audit trail, personnel screening, secure development practice attestation).

### Continuing Operations (post-retainer)

The 6-month retainer ends with the workflow shipped and the team trained — but most Tier 1/2 clients have no internal team to keep the system running. Continuing Operations is the optional ongoing tier that lets them keep using the workflow without hiring an in-house operator.

**Tier 1 / Tier 2 — Varholdt AI hosts:** $2,000/month, month-to-month, 30-day cancellation notice. Includes:

- Hosting and infrastructure on Varholdt AI accounts (client's domain points at this hosting)
- API tokens up to a defined monthly cap; pass-through at cost above cap
- Light monitoring and incident triage during business hours
- Bug fixes on shipped workflows
- Prompt tuning and minor adjustments as documents/workflow evolve
- Model-version migrations when an AI provider deprecates a model
- Quarterly review with the workflow owner

**Tier 3 — Client hosts (their GovCloud / their compliance boundary):** $1,500–$2,000/month, month-to-month, 30-day cancellation. No hosting component (they own it). Same scope otherwise: advisory, prompt tuning, bug fixes, model migrations, quarterly review.

**Excluded from Continuing Operations:**

- 24/7 on-call or uptime SLA
- New workflows or features (quoted separately as a new sprint or build)
- Strategic decisions the client should be making
- All retainer-level exclusions still apply (see Part 3)

**Clean-handoff terms (when client cancels):**

- Source code transferred to a repo the client owns
- Runbook documenting how the system runs and what it costs to run
- 30 days of transition support to either help them migrate to a SaaS alternative or hand off to another consultant
- No promise that the system continues to operate unsupervised after handoff. The client is releasing the operator, not buying a perpetual-motion machine.

**Sales discipline:**

- Discussed at month 5 of the retainer, not earlier. Premature talk creates exit-anxiety.
- Default assumption for Tier 1/2 clients: they will take it, because there is no internal team to receive a handoff.
- The retainer SOW reserves the right to discuss Continuing Operations at month 5; nothing is committed in advance.

## Part 11 — Risks and How They're Handled

1. **Doc-rewriting addiction.** Biggest risk. The pattern of rewriting the founding doc instead of executing it has already happened five times. Rule: this is the last rewrite before a buyer conversation. Subsequent revisions only after 50 outreach attempts have been made.

2. **No conversations after 30 outreach attempts.** Diagnosis order: (1) target list quality, (2) outreach message, (3) offer. Do not jump to (3). Re-examine (1) and (2) first with a specific friend or advisor who can read the messages.

3. **First sprint client provides no access.** Mitigation: SOW requires sample documents and 2–4 stakeholder interviews. If client cannot or will not provide these in Day 1 kickoff, the sprint pauses until they can.

4. **Scope creep.** One workflow per sprint. Fixed SOW. New requests classified as bugs (fix), change requests (out of scope), or new SOWs (quote separately). No exceptions, including for friends.

5. **AI cost surprises.** Pass-through above cap. Monitor weekly. Recalculate caps after sprint #1 against real workflow data.

6. **FDEs from AI labs.** Not a near-term threat for the ICP. Anthropic and OpenAI FDEs target Fortune 500 and well-funded mid-market. The 40-person SDVOSB and the 80-person regional insurance agency are not their customers and won't be for years.

7. **SaaS commoditization of generic workflows.** Mitigation: Buy/build/integrate stays a core principle. Recommend SaaS when SaaS fits. Win where workflows are specific, cross-system, human-reviewed, and poorly served by generic tools.

8. **Solo bandwidth crush.** Hard cap at one active sprint OR one active retainer build at a time until delivery process is proven. Do not sign retainer #2 until retainer #1 is past month 2 and stable.

9. **Income volatility.** Keep DOL job until 6 months of family expenses in cash AND meaningful recurring revenue (retainer revenue ≥ $10K/mo with at least one renewed client).

10. **Liability event.** Mitigation: human-in-the-loop on every deployed workflow, audit logs, decision-support framing (never autonomous high-stakes decisions), E&O coverage, contract liability caps.

This document is v5. v1 through v4 were written without a single buyer conversation having taken place. v5 is the last rewrite until one of the following is true:

1. 50 outreach attempts have been made and produced zero discovery calls (then revise the outreach copy and target list, not the offer)

Reading a Twitter screenshot is not on the list. Reading another agent's reply is not on the list. Having a worry on a Tuesday night is not on the list. The doc is good enough. Execute it.

Everything else is logistics.
